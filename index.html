War Truth

Russia's War Against Ukraine

On February 24, 2022, Russia launched a full-scale invasion of Ukraine, marking a significant escalation of the conflict that began in 2014.

The international community condemned the attack and many countries imposed severe sanctions on Russia.

Luhansk Region

The Luhansk region has been one of the major hotspots, witnessing intense fighting and heavy casualties on both sides.

Efforts to secure humanitarian corridors and provide aid continue amid ongoing hostilities.

Donetsk Airport

Donetsk Airport became a symbol of Ukrainian resistance with heroic defenders holding out against overwhelming odds for 242 days.

The term "cyborgs" was coined to honor the courage and resilience of these defenders.

International Support

Ukraine has received military, financial, and humanitarian support from numerous countries, bolstering its defense capabilities.

Global solidarity has been expressed through rallies, sanctions, and diplomatic efforts.

Drones in Modern Warfare

Drones have become one of the most important elements of modern warfare. Ukraine uses both military-grade and civilian drones for reconnaissance, artillery correction, and precision strikes.

FPV drones became a true battlefield revolution — inexpensive, accurate, and capable of destroying armored vehicles.

Ukrainian volunteers and engineers now produce thousands of drones every month. Unmanned systems play a crucial role in destroying tanks, ammunition depots, electronic warfare systems, and enemy bases.

Mariupol and the Defense of Azovstal

Mariupol became one of the most tragic symbols of the war. The city was under siege for more than 80 days — without water, electricity, or humanitarian corridors.

Russian forces systematically destroyed residential districts, hospitals, and civilian shelters — including the theater where hundreds of people were killed.

The defenders of Azovstal — the Azov Regiment, marines, border guards, and police — held out even under complete encirclement. Despite shortages of ammunition, food, and medicine, they continued to fight with extraordinary perseverance.

Azovstal became a global symbol of bravery. The defenders resisted overwhelming enemy forces and became an example of courage for the whole world.
